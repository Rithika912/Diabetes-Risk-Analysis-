# 🩺 Diabetes Risk Analysis | Python EDA Project

## 📌 Project Overview
Analysed a clinical diabetes dataset to classify patients into 
Low, Medium, and High risk categories using key health indicators 
such as fasting glucose, HbA1c, BMI, and postprandial glucose levels.

## 🛠️ Tools & Libraries Used
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- Seaborn & Matplotlib (Data Visualisation)
- Jupyter Notebook

## 🎯 Objective
To identify patterns and risk factors associated with diabetes 
diagnosis using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) techniques.

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Patients aged **51–60** showed the highest fasting glucose levels
- **BMI > 30** combined with **fasting glucose > 126 mg/dL** 
  strongly indicates diabetes risk
- **HbA1c and fasting glucose** emerged as the top correlated 
  risk factors across 10+ clinical variables
- Patients with **less physical activity** and **poor sleep patterns** 
  showed higher diabetes diagnosis rates
- Risk classification: Score < 4 → Low | 5–7 → Medium | > 8 → High

## 📊 Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning & EDA (shape, info, null check, describe)
- Age interval segmentation (18–30, 31–40, 41–50, 51–60, 60+)
- Risk level classification (Low / Medium / High)
- Correlation heatmap across clinical variables
- Glucose threshold flagging (Fasting > 126, Postprandial > 200)
- Gender-wise analysis of glucose, insulin & HbA1c levels
- Sleep & physical activity vs diabetes diagnosis

## 📁 Dataset
Clinical diabetes dataset with 10+ health variables including 
glucose levels, BMI, HbA1c, blood pressure, cholesterol, 
and lifestyle indicators.
